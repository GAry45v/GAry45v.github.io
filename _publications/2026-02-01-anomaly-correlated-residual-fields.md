This work studies unsupervised cross-domain anomaly detection under significant domain shift. The key idea is to model residual evolution during reverse diffusion as a field signal, separating anomaly-aligned non-stationary drift from normal sample variation.

The method constructs residual, directional, and path-integral field features, then aligns field statistics across domains through temporal reparameterization, direction subspace projection, and second-order whitening. This improves robustness when target-domain anomaly labels are unavailable.
