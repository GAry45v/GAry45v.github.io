This series studies Agentic RL as behavior learning for AI agents. Instead of focusing only on final answers, it asks how models should learn from trajectories: states, actions, tool calls, observations, feedback, recovery, and stopping decisions.

Topics include:

- the difference between RLHF, reasoning RL, and Agentic RL
- reward design for long-horizon tasks
- credit assignment across multi-step trajectories
- verifier-based training for math, coding, and tool-use tasks
- reward hacking and process-level constraints
- evaluation of coding agents, browser agents, and research agents
